    <div class="process-step">
      <div class="process-step-num">01 — Define</div>
      <div>
        <h2 class="process-step-title">Define</h2>
        <div class="process-step-sub">Discover and empathize.</div>
        <p class="process-step-body">Defining a problem requires research, and I like to tackle this in stages. I start by collecting feedback from the team, reading user reviews of previous gen products, and checking out user feedback on the competition. This tips me off to products I should test first-hand, users to talk to, or studies to be performed for greater insight. Throughout the process, I share findings through decks, competitive analyses, and user personas to align the team in a common direction.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="process-step">
      <div class="process-step-num">02 — Ideate</div>
      <div>
        <h2 class="process-step-title">Ideate</h2>
        <div class="process-step-sub">Outline the solution.</div>
        <p class="process-step-body">Ideating brings a rough vision of the solution. Post-its and white boards are my favorite places to sketch and refine user workflows and information architecture. I involve key stakeholders in this process to generate the best ideas.</p>

      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="process-step">
      <div class="process-step-num">03 — Prototype</div>
      <div>
        <h2 class="process-step-title">Prototype</h2>
        <div class="process-step-sub">Bring the idea to life.</div>
        <p class="process-step-body">Ideas go from fuzzy to clear in the prototyping stage. Pen and paper is my favorite place to begin. Once the direction is clear, I move to a design program like Sketch to bring the design to a higher fidelity. If the feature is new or hard to explain in a linear flow, I create an interactive prototype using InVision. Throughout this process, I work closely with a writer on interface copy.</p>
        <div class="gallery-full">
          <img :src="'/images/dm-design-iterations2.png'" alt="Iterations of the Device Manager overview screen" />
          <p class="img-caption">Iterations of the Device Manager overview screen</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="process-step">
      <div class="process-step-num">04 — Test</div>
      <div>
        <h2 class="process-step-title">Test</h2>
        <div class="process-step-sub">Do users 'get it'?</div>
        <p class="process-step-body">As soon as there is something tangible to show, I seek out representative users for feedback. Usability tests with targeted end users is the gold standard, but the minimum is a hallway test with a person not close to the project. The testing leads to new iterations and greater confidence in the final design.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="process-step">
      <div class="process-step-num">05 — Build</div>
      <div>
        <h2 class="process-step-title">Build</h2>
        <div class="process-step-sub">Dot the i's and cross the t's.</div>
        <p class="process-step-body">The build stage is where the rubber meets the road. I create software specifications for workflows and UI components that make it clear for developers. I also stay in close contact throughout the development process to QA components and troubleshoot any issues that come up.</p>
        <div class="gallery-full">
          <img :src="'/images/text-box-specs.png'" alt="UI component specification" />
          <p class="img-caption">UI component specification</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="process-step">
      <div class="process-step-num">06 — Analyze</div>
      <div>
        <h2 class="process-step-title">Analyze</h2>
        <div class="process-step-sub">Influence the next generation.</div>
        <p class="process-step-body">Post launch is a great time to take stock of how things went. Product reviews, star ratings, customer support calls, and feedback from the press are all great indicators of how successful a product is. These provide insights for the next product generation.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
